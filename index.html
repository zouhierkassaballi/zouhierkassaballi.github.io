About Me

Behavioral Economist bridging research and real-world impact

I'm a PhD Candidate at the University of Erfurt with 6+ years of experience researching cognitive biases and decision-making architecture. My work focuses on designing large-scale behavioral experiments and translating complex economic theory into actionable digital interventions.

Currently, I'm leading research on how soft commitments, reminders, and loss aversion framing can close the intention-action gap—helping people follow through on their goals in educational and digital contexts.

Based in Göttingen, Germany, I'm passionate about leveraging incentive design and choice architecture to enhance user experience and drive meaningful behavioral change.
